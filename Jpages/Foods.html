TOP 10 Most food Destination for travellers in Philippines.

PEANUT KISSES

While foodies can’t exactly get a sugar high from Bohol’s Chocolate Hills, they can definitely score sweet treats nearby. Bohol actually has an impressive supply of sugary goods like biko, maruya, otap, and kalamay — that many Filipinos love sharing with foreign friends. Peanut Kisses and Boholano Ubi Polvoron work great as pasalubong, too! To add to Bohol’s allure, the tropical destination just recently opened the Panglao International Airport, which makes this paradise more accessible to foreigners. they were inspired by the American chocolate Hershey’s kisses around the time of the Second World War when a plantation in Bohol was trying to figure out what to do with their peanut crop It so happens that Bohol has famous mountain formations that are reminiscent of the shape of sweet kisses candy; in fact, these geographical wonders have been dubbed Chocolate Hills!
